12/4 - 12/8 Week 15
This was the process for the spaghetti bridge.

  1. The first bridge weighed 46.7g and the second bridge weighed 57.4g.
  2. The first bridge held 1241g worth of water and the second held 675g of water
  3. The first bridge held 27 times its body weight and the second held 12 times its body weight. It wasn't as much as we hoped but we were still proud of it
  4. The heaviest part of bridge one was the joints where the spaghetti met, where it looked like the bridge was at its thickest. That was because that was where the spaghetti was most concentrated. FOr the second bridge, I tried to distribute the weight as evenly as possible, but I think that the heaviest part was where I ended up puttting the clips to hang the bucket. That was toward the end of each side, where most of the spaghetti met up.
  5. For the first bridge, we were experimenting and seeing what we could do. We wanted to see how the spaghetti held together and saw that if we made some areas thicker than others, it would be stronger. The second bride, we tried to make use of shapes and create angles that would support it as well as possible. The inspiration for the bridge was seeing triangles on different bridges and seeing what other people in the class were coming up with
  6. The designs did not work as well as we thought it would. We hoped that it would have held more water, but we realized that we would have had to change a lot about each. We still did an OK job, and help quite a bit of water, which was the goal..
  7. The weakest points on each bridge was where I expected it would be. They were the weakest supported areas and the heaviest part of the bridges. On the first bridge, it broke where the spaghetti had holes in it. The bridge also kept breaking in areas before testing, so it was weak from the start. On the second bridge, it broke at the heaviest part, were I thought it would be at its weakest.
  8. I think in order to improve and support the first bridge, we should have included better vertical supports, because the most supported sections were faced horizontally and did not help it that much. We also made it very thin which caused it to not be able to support the bucket as much as we wanted it to. As far as the second bridge, I would have made the width a little thinner and not have as much random placing of sphaghetti. I think that more intentional angles and consistent triangles would have made it way more supportive.
